How these numbers are estimated

  • A scheduled job samples each fleet aircraft's live ADS-B position roughly once per hour and stores only airborne snapshots. Paths connect those hourly points, so they are coarse approximations, not continuous tracks.
  • Airborne time per flight is credited as the observed span between the first and last sample plus one sampling interval, so a helicopter caught by a single scrape is counted as roughly one hour aloft. Real flight time can differ, especially for brief hops.
  • Fuel = estimated airborne hours × a per-model cruise burn (Bell 429 ≈ 50, Bell 412 ≈ 100, Bell 407 ≈ 40 US gal/hr). Cost = fuel × the Jet-A price above. All figures are order-of-magnitude estimates for public interest, not operational or budget data.
  • Fleet identified by FAA tail number → ICAO Mode-S hex. Data via adsb.lol and compatible community aggregators.
